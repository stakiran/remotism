# remotism

## For non-Japanese readers

**Remote should be the default.** *Remotism* is a Japanese book arguing that remote work is the third paradigm of how we work — after live-in arrangements and office commuting — and that the "office vs. remote" debate is unproductive. Remote wins.

The book lays out a framework called **Default Remote**, organized in three layers (3T):

- **Tenet** — the cultures, beliefs, and mental models we have to recognize and change
- **Technique and Thought** — concrete practices and stances for working remotely
- **Technology** — the tools and the "Literal IT Literacy" required to use them

Topics include the structural difference between communication and information sharing, asynchronous-first management, seamless workflows, role division, and the digital literacy that all of this rests on. Coming from Japan — a country famous for its office-first work culture — the book offers a sharp internal critique that may be of interest to readers outside Japan.

The full text is in [`merged.md`](./merged.md). It is written in Japanese, but feel free to paste it into ChatGPT or another LLM to translate, summarize, or ask questions — the author explicitly welcomes this in the afterword.
